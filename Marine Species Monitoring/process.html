Our Process

Our research process is carefully structured to ensure the highest accuracy in species identification and data collection.

  1. Planning and Preparation: Our team identifies specific marine environments and species that require monitoring. We coordinate with local authorities, researchers, and environmental groups to define research objectives.
  2. Deployment: Using advanced underwater drones equipped with high-definition cameras, sonar, and sensors, we collect footage and data on marine species in real-time. The drones can explore various depths, from shallow coral reefs to the deep sea.
  3. Data Analysis: Once data is collected, we utilize advanced algorithms and AI technologies to identify species, map habitats, and analyze behavioral patterns. The processed data is then verified by marine biologists.
  4. Results and Reporting: We share our findings with research institutions, conservation organizations, and government agencies, contributing to the global database on marine biodiversity. Results are also made available to the public through open-access platforms.
